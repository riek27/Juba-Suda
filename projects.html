Featured Project: Juba Community Center

Project Overview

This community-focused project in Hai Referendum, Juba, involved installing a large-scale bio-digester system to serve a neighborhood community center and surrounding households. The system was designed to handle the waste from community events, daily activities, and nearby residences.

The installation has significantly improved sanitation in the area, eliminating the need for frequent septic tank emptying and reducing environmental contamination. The treated water is used for community garden irrigation, creating a circular economy of waste management.

Key Features:

  • 2000-liter capacity bio-digester system
  • Biogas collection for community kitchen
  • Water treatment for irrigation
  • Educational signage about sustainable sanitation

Project Results

Key Achievements

  • • 90% reduction in waste disposal costs for the community
  • • Elimination of foul odors in the neighborhood
  • • Production of biogas used for community kitchen
  • • Creation of a sustainable irrigation source
  • • Improved public health indicators in the area
